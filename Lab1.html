Abstract on Research

The media has immense influence over people, at times indirectly dictating what they should or
should not do in their life. From newspapers, libraries, and word of the mouth we can receive information.
However, predominantly with just a touch of a mobile screen, we can access everything and with this,
consequently comes censorship. Generally, censorship occurs in a variety of ways; Books, films, music,
the press, radio, television, and the entirety of the internet for many claimed reasons such as national
security and individual protection. However, with suspicion fueled by skepticism of this, I decided to
research this topic. The ethical dilemma in question here is regarding the moral principle of the general
media suppressing the masses that use their programs. To explore if it is ethical for the media to censor
information, we will discuss the two perspectives in this dilemma, the media, and the audience/users.
The first perspective will aim to investigate the media giants and their standpoint on why censoring
is a necessity for their user regulations. The second perspective will follow the audience/users and
their opinions on why censoring is immoral, supporting individual standards in place of society.
All of this is in significance to finding a solution that puts both sides at ease.
